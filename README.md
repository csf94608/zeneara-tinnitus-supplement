## How Does Zeneara Work? The GABA-Glutamate Connection

Here's where things get interesting. In 2024, researchers at Harvard, the University of Zurich, and the American Tinnitus Association published findings pointing to a **chemical imbalance in the brain's auditory region** as a root cause of tinnitus.

What kind of imbalance?

Your brain has two key chemicals fighting for control: **GABA** (the calming one) and **glutamate** (the excitatory one). Think of GABA as the brake pedal and glutamate as the gas. In people with tinnitus, GABA levels are too low. Glutamate runs unchecked. The auditory part of your brain gets stuck in overdrive — firing signals that don't correspond to any real sound.

That's the noise you hear. Your brain, idling too high.

Zeneara's formula is built around boosting GABA activity. Taurine, the probiotic strains (*Lactobacillus paracasei* and *Lactobacillus brevis*), and valerian all play roles in GABA production or receptor sensitivity. Ginkgo biloba (maidenhair tree extract) improves circulation to the inner ear. Zinc and vitamin B6 support overall nerve health. Passion flower extends the calming effect.

Will it work for everyone? No. That's not how bodies work. But the mechanism makes sense on paper, and it's grounded in real neuroscience — not wishes.
